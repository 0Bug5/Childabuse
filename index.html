WHAT IS ABUSE?
Child abuse is doing something or failing to do something that results in harm to a child.

Types of maltreatment include physical, sexual, or emotional abuse and neglect. It is important to note that each state has its own definitions of child abuse and neglect, which determines how and when investigations are done. This list gives a general overview of typical signs and symptoms for each type of abuse.

If you would like to find out more information about a specific state’s laws related to Child Abuse, USE THIS INTERACTIVE MAP TO FIND STATE INFO. If you would like to talk through the situation first, contact a Childhelp counselor via call, text, or chat link. We are here to help!

MISSION STATEMENT:

Childhelp exists to meet the physical, emotional, educational and spiritual needs of abused, neglected and at-risk children. We focus our efforts on advocacy, intervention, treatment, prevention, family resilience and community outreach..

Frequently Asked Questions

Explore commonly asked questions about our Child Abuse.

1. What is the purpose of the information system?

The Child Abuse to streamline the management and reporting of child abuse cases and incidents.

2. How does the system handle sensitive data?

We ensure the security and confidentiality of sensitive child abuse data through encryption and strict access controls.
